Frequently Asked Questions

```

What is prompt engineering and why does it matter?

Prompt engineering is the practice of designing inputs to AI language models to reliably produce high-quality outputs. A well-engineered prompt can reduce hallucinations by 40–60%, improve output consistency, and dramatically lower token cost by eliminating unnecessary back-and-forth. It's the most impactful skill for anyone building with LLMs.

How do I estimate token count for GPT-4 or Claude?

As a rule of thumb, 1 token ≈ 4 characters or ¾ of a word in English. GPT-4 uses the cl100k_base tokenizer; Claude uses a similar BPE tokenizer. Our Token Estimator calculates this automatically — just paste your prompt and select your model. This matters because both cost and context limits are measured in tokens, not characters.

What is a prompt injection attack and how can I prevent it?

A prompt injection attack is when user-provided text overrides or hijacks your system prompt — for example, a user writing Ignore all previous instructions and.... In production LLM applications, this can expose sensitive data or cause the model to behave unexpectedly. Our Injection Detector scans for known jailbreak patterns, role-override attempts, and adversarial inputs before they reach your model.

What makes a high-quality prompt for GPT-4, Claude, or Gemini?

The five pillars of a quality prompt are: a clear role definition (who the model is), precise task description (what to do), relevant context (background the model needs), explicit constraints (what not to do), and a defined output format (how to respond). Our Quality Analyzer scores your prompt across all five and tells you exactly what to improve.

How do I choose between different prompt lengths?

Prompt length should match task complexity. Simple classification or transformation tasks work best with concise prompts under 200 tokens. Complex reasoning, code generation, or structured output tasks benefit from detailed prompts with examples (200–800 tokens). Beyond 1000 tokens, you risk diluting the model's attention on the most critical instructions. Our Length Analyzer gives model-specific recommendations based on your task type.
